## Project Overview

**Research Context:** Geopolitical tensions in the Middle East and their impact on global energy supply dynamics.

## Key Insights

### Supply Visibility
Real-time inventory tracking (Kpler, Privateer, Kayrros) provides early signals of supply constraints weeks/months before traditional data surfaces. Satellite monitoring reveals operational changes and maintenance schedules in real-time.

### Production Capacity
Rig activity tracking (Enverus) and flaring detection (Kayrros) reveal production capacity constraints and expansion potential. Critical for forecasting supply growth and identifying bottlenecks.

### Demand Signals
Shipping data (Vortexa, MarineTraffic) and grid monitoring (YES Energy) provide real-time demand indicators. Port congestion and vessel movements signal trade flow changes before official statistics.

### Risk Monitoring
Geopolitical data and satellite monitoring enable early identification of supply disruption risks. LNG outage detection and pipeline surveillance provide operational transparency.

### Trading Opportunities
Vessel tracking and shipping analytics enable arbitrage identification in real-time. Freight cost analysis and route optimization inform trading strategy.

The global energy market faces unprecedented volatility driven by:

- **Geopolitical Tensions:** Middle East conflicts directly affecting crude supply and prices

- **Data Explosion:** 1000s of new data sources emerging annually

Alternative data provides institutional investors with real-time signals that traditional financial data cannot deliver. Early-warning indicators enable faster decision-making and competitive advantage.
